The Department of Organic Chemistry was established in the year 2006. The Department strives for high quality teaching and research. It offers a two-year M.Sc. Organic Chemistry programme. Students with B.Sc. (with Chemistry background) and at least 50% marks in aggregate are eligible. The teaching emphasises deep understanding of organic reactions, methodologies, natural products isolation and characterization, biologically active molecules, industrial chemicals and drug-related organic synthesis. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

Vision: To enhance teaching and laboratory facilities with dedicated faculty and to attract best students; to inspire interest in chemistry among women and help them become competent for NET/SLET. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

Mission:

  • Update curriculum periodically with vocational courses, interdisciplinary topics and project work. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}
  • Encourage research collaborations and apply for funding. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}
  • Conduct student seminars, e-learning, reading & learning opportunities. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}

Eligibility

B.Sc. (3 year or 4-year degree) with Chemistry as one of the subjects. Candidates should have secured at least 50% aggregate marks in B.Sc. degree. :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}

Programme Content (CBCS)

The curriculum comprises 96 credits over four semesters. Core and elective courses include topics from Inorganic, Organic, Physical, General, Analytical and Spectral Chemistry. From 2020–21, a Project Work in fourth semester is introduced. :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}

External Electives (some):

  • Chemistry for All
  • Polymer Science
  • Chemistry of Drugs and Their Action

Internal Electives (some):

  • General Chemistry-II
  • Chemistry in Contemporary Society
  • Bio-organic Chemistry

Programme / Course Outcomes

The department defines several outcomes for M.Sc. Organic Chemistry including:

  • Synthesize, separate and characterize organic compounds using chemical and instrumental techniques. :contentReference[oaicite:10]{index=10}
  • Design synthetic methods for target-oriented organic compounds of pharmaceutical and industrial importance. :contentReference[oaicite:11]{index=11}
  • Apply theoretical and spectroscopic methods to chemical systems; analyze reaction mechanisms; elucidate structures via UV, IR, NMR, MS. :contentReference[oaicite:12]{index=12}
  • Gain awareness of environmental and societal impact of chemical science; promote green methods & sustainable development. :contentReference[oaicite:13]{index=13}
